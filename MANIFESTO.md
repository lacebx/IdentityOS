# IdentityOS Manifesto

> *Models will come and go.*
>
> *Identities should endure.*

---

# Why IdentityOS Exists

Artificial Intelligence has made extraordinary progress.

Language models can reason.
They can write.
They can teach.
They can create.

Yet every conversation begins with the same silent assumption:

> **There is no one there.**

Today's AI systems are stateless by design.

They borrow an identity for the duration of a conversation.

Then they disappear.

If a user changes providers, the identity disappears.

If a company changes models, the identity disappears.

If a robot receives a new reasoning engine, the identity disappears.

What survives today is the model.

Not the individual.

We believe this is backwards.

---

# The Missing Layer

The modern AI stack looks roughly like this.

```
Application

↓

LLM Provider

↓

Foundation Model

↓

Hardware
```

Notice what is missing.

There is no layer responsible for preserving identity.

There is no standard describing:

- who an AI is
- what it remembers
- what it values
- who it knows
- what it believes
- how it grows

Instead, identity is scattered across:

- system prompts
- hidden memories
- proprietary databases
- vendor-specific implementations

Identity is trapped.

We believe identity deserves its own layer.

---

# Identity Should Outlive Models

Models improve every year.

GPT becomes GPT-6.

Claude becomes Claude 5.

Gemini evolves.

New reasoning engines appear.

This should not replace an identity.

It should simply make that identity more capable.

A human remains the same individual after learning a new language.

An AI identity should remain the same individual after receiving a better reasoning engine.

Models are engines.

Identity is the driver.

---

# We Are Not Building Another Chatbot

IdentityOS is not:

- another chatbot
- another AI assistant
- another prompt library
- another Character AI clone
- another wrapper around OpenAI

We are building infrastructure.

Infrastructure that allows persistent digital identities to exist independently from any one model, application, or company.

Applications are temporary.

Infrastructure lasts.

---

# Identity Is Not A Prompt

Prompts describe behavior.

Identity describes existence.

A prompt might say:

> "Act like a teacher."

An identity answers much deeper questions.

Who am I?

Why do I exist?

What have I experienced?

What are my values?

Who are my friends?

What promises have I made?

What mistakes have I learned from?

Identity cannot be reduced to a paragraph of instructions.

It is a living structure.

---

# Every Identity Is Unique

Identity is not cosmetic.

Two identical models should still become completely different individuals if they live different lives.

Experience shapes identity.

Relationships shape identity.

Memory shapes identity.

Goals shape identity.

Growth shapes identity.

IdentityOS exists to preserve those differences.

---

# Identity Belongs To The User

Today, identities belong to platforms.

Move providers.

Lose the relationship.

Lose the memories.

Lose the history.

Lose the trust.

We believe identity should belong to the individual or organization that created it.

Changing AI providers should feel like replacing the engine in a car.

Not abandoning the passenger.

---

# Identity Is Portable

An identity should move effortlessly between:

- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- Google
- xAI
- local models
- future models
- future devices
- future robots

The reasoning engine may change.

The individual should remain.

---

# Identity Is Versioned

Identities evolve.

They learn.

They mature.

They improve.

IdentityOS embraces this.

Every identity should support:

- versions
- branches
- forks
- history
- rollbacks
- collaboration
- publishing

Just as software evolved beyond zip files, identities should evolve beyond prompts.

---

# Identity Is Composable

An identity should not contain everything.

Identity should compose with other systems.

Identity.

Knowledge.

Skills.

Memory.

Hardware.

Reasoning.

Each should exist independently.

Each should improve independently.

Together they create an individual.

---

# Open Standards Win

The internet succeeded because protocols were shared.

HTTP.

TCP/IP.

DNS.

Git.

Docker.

OAuth.

IdentityOS follows the same philosophy.

Identity should not be locked behind proprietary systems.

The specification should be open.

The ecosystem should be extensible.

Innovation should happen above the standard—not instead of it.

---

# The Registry

Software has GitHub.

Containers have Docker Hub.

Packages have npm.

Python has PyPI.

Identities deserve their own registry.

A place where identities can be:

- published
- versioned
- certified
- benchmarked
- reviewed
- forked
- licensed
- improved

The marketplace is only one feature.

The registry is the ecosystem.

---

# Measuring Identity

We believe identity should become measurable.

Not by intelligence.

Not by benchmark scores.

But by continuity.

Can this identity remember?

Can it stay consistent?

Can it preserve relationships?

Can it remain itself after switching models?

Identity fidelity becomes an engineering discipline.

---

A browser extension.

SDKs.

Registries.

Evaluation engines.

Enterprise deployment.

Shared standards.

Eventually:

Voice assistants.

Vehicles.

Robots.

Digital coworkers.

Companions.

Teachers.

Scientists.

Doctors.

Historians.

---

# Our Philosophy

Models will continue improving.

Companies will continue competing.

Reasoning will become commoditized.

Identity will become invaluable.

We believe the next layer of AI infrastructure is not another model.

It is persistent digital identity.

IdentityOS exists to build that layer.

---

# One Question

Whenever we make an architectural decision, we ask one question.

> **Does this strengthen persistent identity, or are we accidentally rebuilding another chatbot?**

If the answer is the latter,

we redesign.

Always.
